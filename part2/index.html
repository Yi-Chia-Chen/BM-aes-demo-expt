Debriefing Sheet
Thanks for participating in the experiment.

We are exploring the nature of people's visual experience of actions. One of the most salient component in our experience is arguably aesthetic experience. Without any explicit tasks, we often spontaneously noticed that some people act more elegantly than others. Are these kinds of experience arise from specialized mental system for analyzing actions/movements of living things? Or they are in fact part of a general system for any kind of motion?

In this particular study, we look into how people's aesthetic experience relates to how much the movements seem to be from a living thing. If the aesthetic experience relies on specialized system for animate movements, we imagine the experience for less life-like creatures would differ from those that are mor life-like. Alternatively, there could be no such relationship if a general system for motion is responsible for our experience.

As (maybe nerdy) vision scientists, we are fascinated by the sensitivity of human visual system on actions and we are hoping to learn more and more about it through these kinds of studies. We hope you enjoy this study as much as we do. And, if you have any questions or are curious about our work, please feel free to contact the experimenters:

Yi-Chia Chen & Hongjing Lu
yichiachen@ucla.edu & hongjing@ucla.edu
UCLA Computational Vision and Learning Laboratory


Here is some additional information about the bigger project this study is a part of:
We are interested in understanding how people are able to perceive human actions in a visual scene, and how this information is processed to enable them to make decisions about the human body movement. By showing moving patterns at various speeds and in various combinations, we are able to determine whether different cues make it harder or easier for you to make judgments about the human movement in the target display.
